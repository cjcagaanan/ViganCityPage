About Vigan

Vigan City, located in Ilocos Sur, Philippines, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its well-preserved Spanish colonial architecture. It is famous for Calle Crisologo, a historic cobblestone street lined with ancestral houses, antique shops, and horse-drawn carriages called kalesas. The city showcases a blend of Filipino and Spanish influences, evident in its culture, cuisine, and traditions. Tourists can explore landmarks such as the Bantay Bell Tower, Plaza Salcedo, and the Vigan Cathedral, or enjoy local delicacies like empanada and bagnet. With its rich history and vibrant heritage, Vigan remains one of the most charming and culturally significant destinations in the Philippines.

Top Attractions

Calle Crisologo

is Vigan’s most popular tourist attraction and is the highlight of a Vigan City tour. The grounds and pavements of this preserved street are made of cobblestones, and both sides are lined with ancestral houses that are reminiscent of old Spanish towns.

Vigan Cathedral

​Also called the Saint Paul Metropolitan Cathedral, the Vigan Cathedral is another important landmark in Ilocos Sur’s capital city and is also included in a Vigan City tour. .

Bantay Church Bell Tower

Nothing else can give you the best view of Vigan than the Bantay Church Bell Tower. The belfry is situated on a hill overlooking the Bantay district in northeast Vigan. .

Crisologo Museum

Crisologo Museum offers a glimpse of the life led by another Ilocano statesman, Congressman Floro S. Crisologo who was assassinated while attending church services at St. Paul’s Cathedral.

Pagburnayan Jar Factory

Pagburnayan Jar Factory, one of the very few remaining jar factories around Vigan. It’s a popular must-try cultural activity that is also part of a Vigan City tour.

Plaza Burgos

Located within a good walking distance from Plaza Salcedo, this plaza is named after Padre Jose Burgos, whose statue stands as a reminder of the priest’s martyrdom in fighting the Spanish regime. Here, you can take pictures, go people watching, or simply give your tired legs some resting time after a long walk around the area.

Baluarte Zoo

Not too far from the center of Vigan City, you’ll find the Baluarte Resort and Mini Zoo sprawling on roughly 100 hectares of rolling plains and hills. The zoological park belongs to local politician Chavit Singson who used his property to share his love for safari to the public.

Dancing Fountain Show at Plaza Salcedo​

The park between Vigan Cathedral and the Provincial Capitol turns from a haven for picture taking at day to a stage of dancing lights at night that everyone can enjoy for free. Plaza Salcedo features an outdoor light and water show that’s comparable to those you might see in other countries.

Events

Vigan City Fiesta and Longganisa Festival

Annually in late January, with the main events typically culminating on January 22.

This festival honors St. Paul the Apostle, Vigan's patron saint, and celebrates the city's rich history and its renowned longganisa. The 2025 festivities are expected to follow a similar schedule, with events leading up to January 22.

Viva Vigan Binatbatan Festival of the Arts

First week of May.

This festival celebrates Vigan's heritage, particularly its weaving industry, and aims to preserve and promote Ilocano culture.

Tres de Mayo Festival

May 3

A religious and cultural celebration in honor of Apo Lakay (Sto. Cristo Milagroso) at Simbaan a Bassit.

Raniag Vigan Twilight Festival

Late October to early November

A festival of lights with lantern parades, candle float processions, and Halloween-themed events.

Vigan Christmas Fantasy

Entire month of December

Features a grand lighting ceremony, Christmas bazaar, fireworks, and the "Pailaw sa Vigan" light show.
